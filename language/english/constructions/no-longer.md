No longer means that something is finished. It goes in mid-position.

_You can’t buy these bikes now. They no longer make them._  
_I used to belong to the sports club, but I'm no longer a member._

No longer can be a little formal. In informal speech we use _not... any longer_ or _not... any more_. Any longer/any
more comes at the end.

_They don't make these bikes any longer/any more._
_Rita has moved. She doesn't live here any longer/any more._
