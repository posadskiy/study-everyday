When there are two short actions, one straight after the other, we can use an -ing form for the first action.

_Opening the bottle, Mike poured the drinks._ (= He opened the bottle and then poured the drinks.)  
_Turning right into Madison Avenue, the car drove north for two blocks._

We can also use the perfect -ing form.

_Having opened the bottle, Mike poured the drinks._ (= After opening the bottle, Mike poured the drinks.)

If either of the actions is long, we **must** use the perfect.

_Having photocopied all the papers, Sarah put them back in the file._  
_Having repaired the car, Tom took it out for a road test._  
~~Repairing the car, Tom took it out for a road test.~~

These patterns are typical of written English. In spoken English, to talk about one action after another, we use a
sentence like this.

_Tom repaired the car and then took it out for a road test._
