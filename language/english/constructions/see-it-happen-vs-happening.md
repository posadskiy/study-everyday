This is about being a part of whole process or observing a part of it.

_We saw Alex plant the tree._ (= He planted the tree. We saw him do the whole job.)  
_We saw Alex planting the tree._ (= He was planting the tree. We saw him in the middle of the job.)  
_I watched Nick light a cigarette._ (Whole process)  
_I watched Nick smoking a cigarette._ (Somewhere in the middle)  
_We noticed a young man sit down and order a meal_ (Whole process of ordering)  
_We noticed a young man sitting at the table eating a meal._ (Just look on him in the process)

When we talk about a short action, it often does not matter which structure we use.

_They heard a car turn/turning the corner._  
_I didn’t see anyone leave/leaving any litter._

_We had to stand in a queue waiting for the bank to open._ (not opening)  
_You can't sit watching television all day._  
_All afternoon, Vicky lay on the sofa thinking about life._  
_Matthew injured his knee doing gymnastics._ (= He injured his knee while he was doing gymnastics.)  
_I went to sleep listening to a podcast._
