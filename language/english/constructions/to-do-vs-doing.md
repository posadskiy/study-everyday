### like

Like + to when we prefer to do something even though we may not enjoy it.

_I like to check my work carefully before I send it in._

like + -ing form for talking about hobbies and interests.

_Claire likes skiing._  
_I don't like swimming much._

### remember and forget

**remember/forget + to do** for necessary actions. The remembering is before the action.

_I must remember to post this letter today. It's important._  
_The clothes are still dirty because I forgot to switch on the machine._

**remember/forget + doing** for memories of the past. The action is before the remembering.

_I can remember posting the letter. I posted it on Friday morning._  
_I'll never forget flying over the Grand Canyon. It was wonderful._

### mean

Mean + to do something = to intend to do it.

_I think Nick meant to break that glass. It didn't look like an accident._

Means doing something expresses the idea of one thing resulting in another.

_I’m applying for a visa. It means filling in this form._

### regret

Regret to do something means to be sorry for something you are doing, e.g. giving bad news.

_We regret to inform you that we are not taking on any new staff at present._

Regret doing something means to be sorry because of something that happened in the past.

_I regret spending all that money. I’ve got none left._

### try

Try to do something = attempt something, to do your best.

_I'm trying to run this computer program._

Try doing something = to do something which might solve a problem.

_I tried clicking on the box, but it doesn’t work._

### stop

**stop + to do** = to cease or quit an ongoing activity.

_She stopped to smoke._ = She was doing something else, but she paused that activity in order to smoke.
_They stopped to talk when the teacher entered._ = They paused what they were doing in order to have a conversation.

**stop + doing** = to pause or interrupt one activity in order to do something else.

_She stopped smoking._ = She quit the habit of smoking.  
_They stopped talking when the teacher entered._ = They were talking, but then they ceased talking.

### go on

**go on + to do** = to proceed to do something new or move on to the next action after completing something else.

_The teacher introduced herself and went on to explain about the course._

**go on + doing** = continue doing something that is already in progress.

_The teacher told everyone to be quiet, but they just went on talking._
