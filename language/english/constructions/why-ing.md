The -ing form is used to indicate the reason for something in a more concise or informal way, instead of using full
clauses like "because" or "since."

`main action + (comma) + -ing form`

_She left the party because she was feeling tired._  
-ing form: _She left the party feeling tired._  
Here, _feeling tired_ explains why she left the party.

_He apologized because he realized he was wrong._  
-ing form: _He apologized, realizing he was wrong._  
_Realizing he was wrong_ explains why he apologized.

### perfect form

_Having spent all his money, Daniel couldn't afford a new jacket._  
_We decided not to travel, having heard the terrible weather forecast._
