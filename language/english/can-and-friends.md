## Ability & Possibility & ...

#### can

- ability - He can move the stone
- possibility 
  - positive - He can get access to the website with his admin permissions
  - negative (impossible) - It can't be yours
- future suggestion - We can go to the Chinese restaurant 
- permission 
  - ask - Can I take ... ?
  - give - You can take ...
  - refuse - You can't take ...
- request - Can you keep me informed?
- offer 
  - We can give you a lift
  - Can I get a taxi for you?

can have

- prediction - You’ve only spent five minutes on that job. You can't have done it properly (= I realize it is impossible that you did the job properly)

  - positive, situation makes something necessary - You have to work tomorrow

- to-infinitive - He doesn't want to have to work from office (~~must~~)

Mainly Present

need to
: about necessity

- necessity (negative) 
  - present
    - You needn't wash those glasses. They're clean
    - You don't need to wash those glasses. They're clean.
  - past
    - we use didn't need to when something was not necessary - Mark didn't need to hurry. He had lots of time.
    - we use needn't have + a past participle for something we did which we now know was not necessary, e.g. booking 
    a table - Alex and Laura booked a table for dinner. But the restaurant was empty. They needn't have booked a table.
    - when the action happened, even though it was not necessary - Mark didn't need to hurry, but he drove at top speed. 
    He likes driving fast
