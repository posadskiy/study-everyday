Third conditional is about hypothetical situations in the past that did not happen. It reflects on how things might have
been different if certain conditions had been met. Essentially, it is used to express regret or speculation about past
events.

`If + past perfect, + would have + past participle`

_If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam._ (I did not study hard, and as a result, I did not pass the exam. This sentence reflects on what might have happened differently if I had studied harder.)
_If she had known about the meeting, she would have attended._ (She did not know about the meeting and did not attend. This reflects on the possible outcome if she had known.)
_If we had left earlier, we would have arrived on time._ (We left late and arrived late. This sentence speculates on arriving on time if we had left earlier.)

### modal verbs
would have, could have and might have.

_If I'd known the result, I could have contacted you._
_We just caught the train. If we'd stopped to buy a paper, we might have missed it._
