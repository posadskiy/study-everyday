Condition is likely, the result is almost 100% sure (not 100% because future is uncertainly)

`If + present simple, + will + base verb`

_If it rains tomorrow, I will stay home._ (There’s a possibility of rain tomorrow, and if it happens, I plan to stay
home.)  
_If you study hard, you will pass the exam._ (If you put in the effort to study, the likely outcome is that you will
pass.)  
_If she invites me, I will go to the party._ (If she decides to invite me, I will choose to attend the party.)

### usage
#### suggestions & warnings
realistic situations

_If you need a ticket, I can get you one._  
_If you feel like seeing the sights, we can take a bus tour._

_If you go on like this, you will make yourself ill._  
_If you don't apologize, I will never speak to you again._

#### polite request

In a polite or formal setting, where the speaker is gently asking for patience.

`If + will + base verb, + will + base verb`

_If you will just wait a moment, I will find someone to help you._ (= Please wait a moment...)

### tenses

#### Present Continuous

_If we're expecting visitors, the flat will need a good clean._

#### Present Perfect

_If you’ve finished with the vacuum cleaner, I will put it away._

### modal verbs
will, can, might, should

_If you haven't got a television, you can’t watch it, can you?_  
_If Henry jogs regularly, he might lose weight._  
_If Matthew is going to a job interview, he should wear a tie._

### order

The if-clause usually comes first, but it can come after the main clause.

_If I hear any news, I'll phone you._  
_I’ll phone you if I hear any news._
