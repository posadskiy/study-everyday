**Second conditional** describes a situation that is unlikely, imaginary, or contrary to fact, and the result that would
happen if that situation were true **in present or future**.

`If + past simple, + would + base verb`

_If I won the lottery, I would buy a big house._ (Winning the lottery is unlikely or imaginary, but if it happened,
buying a big house would be the result.)  
_If she were taller, she would play basketball._ (She is not tall, but if she were, she would play basketball.)  
_If they studied harder, they would get better grades._ (They do not study hard currently, but if they did, their grades
would improve.)

### usage
#### offers & suggestions
hypothetical situations

_If you needed a ticket, I could get you one._
_If you felt like seeing the sights, we could take a bus tour._

### tenses
#### Past Continuous

_If Rachel was playing music, it would not be so quiet in here._

### was vs were 

- **were** is traditionally used for all subjects (I, he, she, it, etc.) in formal English:  
_If I were you, I would take the job._ (This is correct and formal.)

- **was** with "I, he, she, it" for informal speech:  
_If I was rich, I would travel the world._ (Informal, but commonly used.)

### modal verbs
would, could or might.

_If we had a calculator, we could work this out a lot quicker._  
_If Rachel worked harder, she might do even better at her studies._
