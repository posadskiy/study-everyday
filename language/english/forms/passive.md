## Passive tenses

| Tense              | Verb                          | Example                        |
|--------------------|-------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Present Simple     | is/are + verb                 | The bread is baked here        | 
| Present Continuous | is/are + being + verb         | The bread is being baked       |
| Present Perfect    | is/are + being + verb         | The bread is being baked       |
| Past Simple        | was/were + verb               | The bread was baked yesterday  |
| Past Continuous    | was/were + being + verb       | The bread was being baked      |
| Past Perfect       | had + been + verb             | The bread had been baked       |
| Future             | will be + verb                | The bread will be baked next   |
|                    | is/are + going to + be + verb | The bread is going to be baked |
|                    | should + be + verb            | The bread should be baked soon |
|                    | ought to + be + verb          | The bread ought to be baked    |

#### I was given

Using for getting smth from somewhere

_She was given some flowers_ - from someone unnamed  
_My wife is paid more than I am_ - don't need to specify who paid money to wife  
_He has been awarded a prize for his essay_

We can use the following verbs in this structure: `allow, award, feed, give, grant, hand, leave (in a will), lend, offer,
owe, pay, promise, sell, send, show, teach`

#### It is said that

We can use the structure it + passive verb + clause with verbs of reporting.
We use this structure when we cannot say or do not need to say who the speaker is, for example in news reports.

_It is said that Henry is in love with Claire_ (= People say that Henry is in love with Claire)

#### He is said to

This structure is also used in news reports.

_Henry is said to be in love with Claire._

#### Have/Get something done

Have - formal, get - more informal
Means something is done by someone
All forms

_Claire had the room decorated._ = A decorator did the work  
_Mark doesn't have his suits cleaned at Fastclean._  
_Do you have your car serviced regularly?_  
_Where did you have your hair cut?_

_Laura got her shoes repaired._  
_We’re getting the carpet cleaned._  
_Where did you get your hair cut?_  
_Do you get your heating checked every year?_

#### Being something

Being met  
_I want to play volleyball. I hope to be chosen for the team._  
_The minister agreed to answer questions. He agreed to be interviewed on television._  
_Famous people get tired of being recognized everywhere they go._

#### Active forms with a passive meaning

The active -ing form after need has a passive meaning.  
_The bicycle needs oiling._ (= The bicycle needs to be oiled.)  
_The windows need cleaning._ (= The windows need to be cleaned.)

We cannot use the passive -ing form here.
~~The bicycle needs being oiled~~

We sometimes use an active to-infinitive when we talk about a job to be done.  
_I’ve got some emails to answer today._  
_We’ve got this bill to pay._

Here we use the active (to answer) because the subject of the sentence (I) is the person who has to do the job. But if
the subject is not a person, then we use the passive infinitive.  
_The emails are to be answered today._  
_The bill is to be paid without delay._  
_All this mess has to be cleared away._  
_The goods have to be shipped._
