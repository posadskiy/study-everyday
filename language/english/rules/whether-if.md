### whether

for alternatives or choices.
**whether** is used when presenting two or more options or when discussing an uncertain situation. It often implies the
choice between doing something or not doing it.

_We need to decide whether to stay or leave._ (Two choices: stay or leave)
_I'm not sure whether he will come._ (Implying either he will come or he won’t)
_Let me know whether you’re available._

### if

for conditional statements.
**if** is mainly used for conditional situations, where something happens only if a condition is met. It is also used in
reported yes/no questions but typically not when you're explicitly offering two alternatives.

_We will go to the beach if it is sunny._ (Conditional: only if it is sunny)
_She asked if you were coming._ (Indirect question)
